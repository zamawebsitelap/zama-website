## Background

The National Democratic Club has served Capitol Hill since 1953. Founded by former members of the Truman administration and located at 30 Ivy Street SE, it sits within blocks of the Congressional office buildings, the Library of Congress, and the Supreme Court.

The Club had standing. It needed communications that felt current enough to attract and retain the next generation of members.

## The work

This is an ongoing communications and event partnership.

Email marketing and newsletters carry the member relationship week to week. Brand and creative keep the Club's voice consistent and current. Event strategy shapes the annual after party, including the sponsorship program that funds it and sets its tone. The full program is measured against membership and renewals.

## The After Party

What began as an event at the Club has become the after party for the Congressional Baseball Game, drawing over 1,500 guests to Capitol Hill each June and closing Ivy Street to do it.

The most recent edition included more than 15 sponsors, including The Macallan and a McLaren activation on site. That sponsor quality reflects the broader result: the Club has become a room people want to be seen in.
